## D vs E temperature behavior

D_discrete (row 3) spreads out less with increasing temperature than E_discrete (row 1). This is expected: D = C × E, and C (coherence) decreases at high temperature as the text becomes less fluent. The C factor compensates for the fact that incoherent text is inherently harder to predict — high E at high temperature partly reflects incoherence rather than genuine diversity, and D corrects for this.

The E_fit and D_fit rows (rows 2 and 4) are dominated by the exponential fitting degeneracy (see efit_vs_ediscrete caption) and are not yet useful for temperature analysis. The outliers with E_fit in the tens of thousands compress all other points to the x-axis.
